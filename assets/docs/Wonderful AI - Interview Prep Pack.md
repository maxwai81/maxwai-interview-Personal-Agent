## 2. Mirko Zhao – 1st Interview Context

**Profile:** To-be Hong Kong General Manager at Wonderful AI. Previously at Crypto.com (Crypto/Blockchain). Met founders in Italy during strategic meeting with major Italian telecom; joined Wonderful recently.

**Key points from Mirko:**

### Wonderful’s differentiation (3 buckets)
1. **People** – Talent-driven; many FDEs and Deployment Strategists are ex-founders; strong ownership; “everyone is like a mini entrepreneur”
2. **Product** – Agent Builder 2.0 for orchestration; company-branded UI, charts, analytics on the fly; fast pace of improvement
3. **Business model** – No seat-based pricing; run **production-grade pilots for free**; show value first, then expand. “We don’t throw free software – we want strategic conversation, bring in CXO, show commitment, then we build for free.”

### Operating model (Palantir-style forward deploy)
- Per country: **1 GM + 1 Field CTO** → under CTO: **Delivery pods**
- Delivery pod: **1–2 Deployment Strategists** + Forward Deploy Engineers
- Deployment Strategist = **PM + architect + entrepreneur** in one

### Deployment Strategist – day-to-day
- **Scoping & discovery:** Demos, requirements, brainstorming workshops, help find valuable use case (clients often say “board wants AI but we don’t know what”)
- **Commercial:** Work with GM on KPIs, timeline, contract
- **Implementation:** On-site 2–3x/week; plan APIs, integrations, deployment; work with FDEs and CTO; weekly progress/blocker reports
- **Post-launch:** Measure impact, scale from first use case
- **Asia nuance:** Labor cost low (e.g. Guangzhou 4–5k RMB/month) → cost-cutting alone weak; pivot to outbound sales, upsell, new capabilities

### HK status
- Pilot already kicked off with **one of the top enterprises in Hong Kong**

### Hiring bar
- No junior hires; senior only; lots of scope and autonomy; many ex-founders

## 5. Tailoring for Next Steps

### Cover letter / positioning
- Lead with **AI agent and workflow automation** (n8n, Flowise, Cursor)
- Emphasize **ownership:** “I’ve owned customer delivery from discovery through expansion”
- Connect **HR domain** to Wonderful’s HR vertical
- Show **startup fit:** “I’m drawn to owning P&L and delivery at an AI-native company”
- Add **technical proof:** “Built production AI agents and workflow automation that reduced overhead and improved lead qualification”

### Interview prep focus
1. **Ownership & P&L** – Examples where you owned outcomes, not just presales; include metrics
2. **End-to-end delivery** – Discovery → workflow mapping → design → implementation/demo → adoption
3. **AI agent & workflow design** – RFP agent, n8n/Flowise workflows, Workday AI demos

### Questions to ask (updated with 1st interview context)
1. How is the HK delivery pod structured today? Who are the FDEs and CTO you’d work with?
2. What does “measured on customer P&L outcomes” mean in practice for the pilot you’ve kicked off?
3. For the top enterprise pilot in HK – what’s the use case, timeline, and success criteria?
4. How does Wonderful adapt agents to Cantonese, Mandarin, and local regulations?
5. What does the typical path look like from first engagement to production, given the “free pilot then expand” model?
