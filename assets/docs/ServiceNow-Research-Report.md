## 1. Company Background

### Mission & Value Proposition
- **Tagline:** "The world works with ServiceNow™" / "Put AI to Work"
- **Purpose:** "Making the world work better for everyone"
- **Value proposition:** Cloud-based enterprise software platform delivering IT operations, field service management, and app engine solutions. ServiceNow positions itself as an AI platform that unifies tools, systems, and data to automate workflows across the enterprise.

### History & Founding
- **Founded:** November 5, 2003, as Glidesoft, Inc.; formally incorporated in California in 2004
- **Founder:** Fred Luddy (age 49 at founding)
- **Founding team:** Fred Luddy, David Loo, Don Goodliffe, Bow Ruggeri, Patrick Casey
- **Origin:** Started with a single laptop at Luddy's desk in his house in San Diego, California
- **IPO:** June 29, 2012 — raised $209.7M at valuation of ~$2.17B
- **HQ:** Santa Clara, California (relocated after IPO)

### Founding Vision (from Luddy)
Three technological insights drove the founding:
1. Simplified platform for creating business applications
2. Browser-based delivery (novel in 2004)
3. Software as a service vs. on-premise

Initial focus was ITSM applications to demonstrate platform capabilities. Early culture emphasized humility, integrity, customer-first thinking, and open communication.

### Market Position
- **Industry:** Enterprise software, cloud-based workflow automation
- **Employees:** ~25,000–29,000 (LinkedIn: 25,075; Wikipedia/PitchBook: 29,187)
- **Revenue (TTM):** ~$13.3B (Dec 2025)
- **Revenue growth:** ~21–24% YoY
- **Market cap:** ~$118–122B (NYSE: NOW)

## 3. Products & Core Business Solutions

### ITSM (IT Service Management)
- **URL:** https://www.servicenow.com/products/it-service-management.html
- **Purpose:** Automates core IT processes; manages incidents, problems, and changes
- **Standards:** Aligns with ITIL
- **Capabilities:** Manages access and availability of services, fulfills service requests, streamlines services
- **Platform:** Uses AI and cloud on ServiceNow AI Platform; unifies tools, systems, and data

### ITOM (IT Operations Management)
- **Purpose:** Uses AIOps, insights, and automation to predict issues and streamline resolutions
- **Value:** Proactive digital operations

### ITBM (IT Business Management)
- **URL:** https://www.servicenow.com/products/business-management.html
- **Purpose:** IT business management and planning

### Core Business Suite (Core Business Solutions)
- **URL:** https://www.servicenow.com/products/business-suite.html
- **Purpose:** Connects employees, suppliers, systems, and data in a single suite
- **Key capabilities:**
  - Case and process management across business functions
  - Task orchestration to route work to the right experts
  - Unified workspaces for employees
  - Dashboard completion to track SLAs and operational analytics
- **Domains:** HR, Workplace, Legal, Finance, Procurement, Supplier management

### How They Fit Together
All products run on the **ServiceNow AI Platform** and integrate with AI agents for automation, productivity, and 24/7 support. Core Business Solutions focuses on non-IT business workflows (HR, Legal, Finance, Procurement, etc.) while ITSM/ITOM/ITBM focus on IT operations and planning.
